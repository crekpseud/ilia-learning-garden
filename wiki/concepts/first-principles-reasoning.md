# First-Principles Reasoning

## Definition

First-Principles Reasoning is a reasoning practice that breaks a complex problem, claim, or system into fundamental truths and rebuilds understanding or solutions from those foundations.

## Why It Matters

For the learning garden, first-principles reasoning names one way to resist outsourced understanding. Instead of accepting a polished summary, inherited analogy, or tool-generated answer, the learner asks what must be true underneath the surface.

This does not mean every topic should be reduced to axioms before it can enter the garden. The value is more practical: when a concept feels important, confusing, or too easy, first-principles questioning can slow the loop enough to reveal whether the learner can reconstruct the idea in their own terms.

The user's reflection connects this to memory-value selection. Before deciding what to remember, the learner may need to ask which questions matter, which assumptions are load-bearing, and which details are only surface decoration.
