# Evergreen Note

## Definition

An evergreen note is a reusable, revisable note that accumulates insight over time and can be recombined with other notes to support future thinking.

## Why It Matters

Evergreen notes give [[wiki/concepts/digital-garden]] a note-level practice. Instead of storing source fragments, the learner writes concept-oriented notes in their own words, links them into an associative ontology, and revisits them as understanding changes.

For this vault, an evergreen note is close to the ideal behavior of a trusted concept note: it should be durable enough to serve as future context, but alive enough to accept correction, refinement, and new links.

The practice should remain in service of learning. When evergreen note-writing becomes polishing, tagging, or linking for its own sake, it starts to drift toward [[wiki/concepts/the-ritualization-trap]].
