# The Ritualization Trap

## Definition

The Ritualization Trap is the failure mode where a knowledge practice becomes a self-optimizing maintenance ritual that distracts from reading, thinking, learning, and making.

## Why It Matters

This garden is deliberately protocol-shaped, link-rich, and metaphor-heavy. Those qualities are useful, but they can become gravity. If the learner starts optimizing statuses, titles, links, prompts, and folder structures instead of using them to think better, the protocol has started to feed on attention.

The concept is especially important around [[wiki/concepts/evergreen-note|Evergreen Note]] practice. Tending notes should deepen understanding. It should not create an obligation to keep every note polished, perfectly atomic, permanently current, or aesthetically satisfying.

The trap does not mean structure is bad. It means structure should earn its keep by improving retrieval, learning, design judgment, or joy.
