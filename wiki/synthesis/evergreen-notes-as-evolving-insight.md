# Evergreen Notes as Evolving Insight

## Core Understanding

[[wiki/concepts/evergreen-note|Evergreen notes]] frame note-taking as a practice of thinking rather than storage. Instead of treating notes as passive archives, the practice turns notes into atomic, concept-oriented expressions of insight that can be revised, linked, and recombined over time.

The packet positions evergreen notes as a concrete method for growing a [[wiki/concepts/digital-garden]]: each note becomes living material that can be tended, connected, and refined. This strengthens [[wiki/synthesis/digital-garden-ethos]] by giving the garden metaphor a more precise unit of work: an evolving note written in the user's own words.

## Why It Matters

For this learning pipeline, evergreen notes clarify the relationship between trusted concept notes and ongoing learning. A concept note in this vault is not merely an extracted keyword; it should become a maintained handle for a reusable idea. Evergreen note practice gives that maintenance a cognitive purpose: writing in one's own words supports understanding, memory, and bottom-up composition.

The same practice also carries risk. If every note must become polished, atomic, densely linked, and perpetually maintained, the garden can fall into [[wiki/concepts/the-ritualization-trap]]. The protocol should preserve the useful discipline of evergreen notes without turning learning into optimization labor.

## Key Ideas

- Evergreen notes are tools for thinking, not passive storage.
- Atomic notes make ideas easier to recombine.
- Concept-oriented notes differ from source-oriented literature notes.
- Dense links create an associative ontology rather than a rigid hierarchy.
- Writing in one's own words supports the generation effect.
- Incremental writing lets larger outputs emerge from smaller maintained notes.
- [[wiki/concepts/note-titles-as-apis|Note Titles as APIs]] gives evergreen practice a naming discipline: stable titles become handles for memory, links, and agents.
- Tending is useful when it deepens insight, but harmful when it becomes ritual maintenance.
