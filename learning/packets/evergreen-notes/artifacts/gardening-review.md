# Gardening Review

## Packet

- Packet: [[learning/packets/evergreen-notes/packet]]
- Operation: `garden.review`
- Status: advisory

## Whole-Garden Meaning

The human-contact artifact confirms that evergreen notes are not just another PKM technique in this garden. They are becoming part of the garden's core metaphor: wikis should be evergreen, knowledge can behave like a plant, and software can also be understood through growth.

This packet strengthens the idea that the garden is not only an archive of learned things. It is a living interface to thought.

## Unexpected Links

- [[wiki/synthesis/llm-wiki-pattern]] becomes stronger through the user's phrase "note titles as APIs." A Markdown wiki is not only storage; each note title can become an invocation handle for future agents and future thinking.
- [[wiki/concepts/digital-garden]] gains a sharper note-level practice: write for yourself first, lower the emotional stakes, and let notes grow through use.
- [[wiki/synthesis/technopastoral-synthesis]] connects through associative ontologies: knowledge is organized by paths and relationships rather than chronology.

## Emerging Narrative

A clear narrative is forming:

```text
Markdown files are durable thought objects
note titles are handles
links create associative ontologies
evergreen practice keeps the handles alive
AI can use the garden because humans have named and tended it
```

This is a good narrative. It is also a little dangerous, because it could seduce the garden into over-designing titles, links, and rituals before enough material exists.

## Protocol Pressure

The reflection suggests two possible future protocol ideas:

- Note titles may need explicit naming guidance once the trusted wiki grows.
- Reading inbox and writing inbox may become first-class workflow areas later, but it is too early to add more folders just because the phrase is attractive.

## Directional Risk

The main risk is the same one now appearing across packets: living-system metaphors feel deeply right to the user. That can be generative, but it can also become aesthetic gravity.

The garden should keep asking: does this metaphor improve retrieval, learning, and design judgment, or does it merely make the system feel organic?

## Proposals

- Treat `Evergreen Note` as a strong candidate for promotion after review.
- Treat `Note Titles as APIs` as a new candidate, but probably not an immediate promotion unless the user feels the phrase has durable value.
- Defer `Epistemic Status` unless a concrete need appears; it risks ritual overhead.
- Defer `Bi-directional Linking` unless it becomes more than a tool affordance.
- Keep watching the plant/software/garden metaphor so it remains useful rather than decorative.

## Promotion Outcome

The user approved promotion of [[wiki/concepts/evergreen-note]], [[wiki/concepts/the-ritualization-trap]], and [[wiki/concepts/note-titles-as-apis]].

That outcome slightly upgrades the risk model: `Note Titles as APIs` is powerful enough to deserve a trusted handle, but it should be watched for over-engineering. The newly promoted [[wiki/concepts/the-ritualization-trap]] should act as the counterweight.

## Follow-Up Proposal

[[wiki/synthesis/technopastoral-synthesis]] still contains a question that treats `Evergreen Note` as a deferred candidate. That trusted note was not part of this packet's approved patch set, so it should be updated only through a future suggested patch or review.
