## Patch Target: [[wiki/concepts/playful-knowledge-practice]]

### Suggested Addition

```markdown
## Visual Symbolism

Playful knowledge practice can use small visual symbols as optional expression aids. Emojis may help with emotional fluency, memory cues, light feedback, and compact affective annotation.

The important constraint is that symbols should invite play rather than enforce performance. If emojis become mandatory status markers, scoring systems, or maintenance rituals, they risk becoming another instance of [[the-ritualization-trap|The Ritualization Trap]].
```

### Rationale

The packet gives concrete educational examples of emojis supporting expression, interpretation, and feedback. This patch should wait until the user confirms that the material actually helped clarify the garden's emoji question.

Emoji use in this garden should begin as a reversible learning-area experiment, not as protocol schema. Symbols may support memory, affect, and play, but they should remain optional until there is evidence that they improve recall or reflection without adding clutter.
