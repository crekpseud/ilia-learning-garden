# Candidate Concepts

## First-Principles Reasoning

- Status: new candidate
- Definition: A reasoning practice that breaks complex problems into fundamental truths and rebuilds solutions from those foundations.
- Why it matters: It names the kind of thinking the protocol may need to protect from AI-assisted shortcutting.
- Possible aliases: reasoning from fundamentals, foundational reasoning, thinking for yourself
- Related existing notes: [[wiki/synthesis/llm-wiki-pattern]], [[wiki/synthesis/digital-garden-ethos]]
- Promotion recommendation: yes, pending human-contact review

## Desirable Difficulties

- Status: new candidate
- Definition: Learning conditions that slow short-term performance but improve long-term retention, retrieval, and transfer.
- Why it matters: It may become a design principle for the human-contact gate: some friction should be preserved because it helps learning.
- Possible aliases: productive difficulty, learning friction, effortful learning
- Related existing notes: [[wiki/synthesis/digital-garden-ethos]], [[wiki/concepts/digital-garden]]
- Promotion recommendation: yes, pending human-contact review
- If existing, suggested patch: N/A

## Active Reconstruction

- Status: new candidate
- Definition: The act of rebuilding understanding through output-side practices such as summarizing from memory, self-testing, synthesis, and explanation.
- Why it matters: It may describe what the human-contact gate is trying to verify before knowledge enters the trusted wiki.
- Possible aliases: reconstructive learning, generation, teach-back, active digestion
- Related existing notes: [[wiki/synthesis/llm-wiki-pattern]], [[wiki/synthesis/digital-garden-ethos]]
- Promotion recommendation: yes, pending human-contact review
- If existing, suggested patch: N/A

## Illusion of Competence

- Status: new candidate
- Definition: The mistake of treating familiarity, fluency, or ease of re-reading as evidence of actual understanding.
- Why it matters: It names a failure mode for AI-assisted learning: polished summaries can feel understood before the learner can retrieve or use the ideas.
- Possible aliases: fluency illusion, familiarity trap, false mastery
- Related existing notes: [[wiki/concepts/the-stream]], [[wiki/synthesis/digital-garden-ethos]]
- Promotion recommendation: maybe
- If existing, suggested patch: N/A

## Cognitive Offloading

- Status: new candidate
- Definition: Delegating mental work to an external tool or environment.
- Why it matters: It can be helpful as scaffolding, but risky when it replaces the learner's own reconstruction and problem-solving ability.
- Possible aliases: outsourced thinking, delegated understanding, externalized cognition
- Related existing notes: [[wiki/synthesis/llm-wiki-pattern]], [[wiki/concepts/digital-garden]]
- Promotion recommendation: maybe
- If existing, suggested patch: N/A

- Status: new candidate
- Definition: A hierarchy of learning objectives that distinguishes recall and comprehension from higher-order analysis, evaluation, and creation.
- Why it matters: It may help classify human-contact artifact depth.
- Possible aliases: learning objective hierarchy, mastery levels

- Status: new candidate
