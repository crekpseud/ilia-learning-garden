## Reflection

Ok, I listened to podcast.
Let me share some notes that could answer these questions.
- I like the idea of spaced repetition. I have experience with Anki. Unfortunately I could not incorporate it as a habit. It was a part of my usual Manic-Depression - Buildup-Burnout cycle. I adopted an everyday Anki session during my High phase a couple of times but could not maintain it for more than a month or so. Now there is some fear of Anki because of the usual crash that follows the High periods. Learning is hard. Remembering is hard. You never even really know what is worth remembering. You also feel bad if you cannot recite text word-to-word. Now, that the year or so have passed I anyway have hard time remembering what I learned so hard back then, so was it even worth it? And so on... At the same time this sort of practice was probably the best of all. I would like to explore the theme of space repetition more. The main question here is - how do you know what is actually worth to remember? Which is related to a first-principles topic.
- That Seductive feeling of mastering a concept (see The Name of The Rose. Bibliophiles in the most brutal sence)
- Illusion of competence. Evaporative nature of learning. Schopenhauer and Seneca warned us not to read too many books and not to watch too many tic-toc videos because we only have so much capacity to remember. So now again, what's worth remembering?
- Architecture in your mind. Second brain in your first brain?
- Learning is actually pulling information out - not putting it in. That was a new idea for me.
- Desirable difficulties. See the notion of Friction in our Protocol
- Cultural push towards ease. Never delegate the understanding... Good but impossible.
- We are paying extras to save us from the problems that delegated understanding brings with it. It's a vicious loop.
- We have only so much cognitive reserve
- Recognizing is not recalling
- Retrieval strength vs storage strength in the brain (CPU vs RAM vs storage? Neuromorphic event-driven computing?)
- How to determine the value of a particular given memory?
- How to determine which question to ask the AI in the first place? (See Aristotle's First Principle and Descartes' Cogito)
