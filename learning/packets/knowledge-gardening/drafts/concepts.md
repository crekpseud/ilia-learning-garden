# Candidate Concepts

## Digital Garden

- Status: existing
- Definition: A topographic information space defined by links, non-linear navigation, and evolving notes.
- Why it matters: It counters streamification by providing an integrative space for personal sense-making.
- Possible aliases: Knowledge Garden, Second Brain, Personal Wiki
- Related existing notes: [[wiki/concepts/digital-garden]], [[wiki/synthesis/digital-garden-ethos]]
- Promotion recommendation: not yet
- If existing, suggested patch: Add the Garden/Stream contrast and the idea that gardens require tending to avoid unplanned hypertext wilderness.

## The Stream

- Status: new candidate
- Definition: An information architecture that collapses relations into a single-track timeline of events.
- Why it matters: It is the dominant mode of social media and feed-based knowledge consumption, but it is inhospitable to timeless research and deep synthesis.
- Possible aliases: Chronological Feed, Event Stream, Stream
- Related existing notes: [[wiki/concepts/digital-garden]], [[docs/adr/0016-use-topographical-reading-with-append-only-process-history]]
- Promotion decision: promoted to [[wiki/concepts/the-stream]]

## Evergreen Note

- Status: new candidate
- Definition: A note that is constantly edited and refined to remain relevant as a reusable building block for thought.
- Why it matters: It shifts attention from final publication to ongoing cultivation.
- Possible aliases: Permanent Note, Crystalline Information
- Related existing notes: [[wiki/concepts/digital-garden]], [[wiki/synthesis/digital-garden-ethos]]
- Promotion decision: deferred

## Epistemic Status

- Status: new candidate
- Definition: Metadata indicating the level of certainty, maturity, or done-ness of a note.
- Why it matters: It helps readers understand whether a note is a seedling, working draft, mature claim, or stable reference.
- Possible aliases: Note Ranking, Growth Stages, Note Maturity
- Related existing notes: [[wiki/synthesis/digital-garden-ethos]]
- Promotion decision: deferred

## Bi-directional Linking

- Status: new candidate
- Definition: Links that make both the destination and source visible, creating two-way contextual navigation.
- Why it matters: It is one of the main mechanisms behind topographical knowledge navigation and networked thought.
- Possible aliases: Backlinks, Two-way Links
- Related existing notes: [[wiki/concepts/digital-garden]], [[wiki/synthesis/llm-wiki-pattern]]
- Promotion decision: deferred

## AI-Assisted Idea Scoring

- Status: new candidate
- Definition: The use of AI to assess raw captures for recurring signals, clusters, readiness, and potential evergreen topics.
- Why it matters: It could help move raw captures toward draft packets, but it risks homogenizing the personal whimsy of the garden.
- Possible aliases: AI Idea Scoring, AI Clustering, Signal Scoring
- Related existing notes: [[wiki/synthesis/digital-garden-ethos]]
- Promotion recommendation: not yet
