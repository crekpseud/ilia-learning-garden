# Candidate Concepts

## Digital Garden

A personal knowledge space where notes are linked, evolving, contextual, and owned by the author rather than arranged primarily as polished chronological publications.

Promotion decision: promoted to [[wiki/concepts/digital-garden]]. This concept is reusable beyond this packet and directly names the vault metaphor we are testing.

Potential aliases:

- personal knowledge garden
- gardened knowledge space
- digital gardening

Related:

- [[wiki/synthesis/llm-wiki-pattern]]
- [[docs/adr/0016-use-topographical-reading-with-append-only-process-history]]

## Topography Over Timelines

The principle that knowledge spaces should be organized around relationships, themes, and navigable context rather than reverse chronology.

Suggested promotion: maybe. It is useful for designing `index.md` and maps of content, but may be too design-pattern-like until more sources reinforce it.

Related:

- [[wiki/synthesis/llm-wiki-pattern]]

## Continuous Growth

The principle that notes should be expected to evolve over time through small revisions, clarification, expansion, and pruning.

Suggested promotion: maybe. It strongly matches the learning loop, but may be better captured as part of Digital Garden unless it recurs in future packets.

Related:

- [[wiki/synthesis/llm-wiki-pattern]]

## Independent Ownership

The principle that a personal knowledge space should be portable and controlled by its owner rather than trapped inside a platform.

Suggested promotion: maybe. This directly supports the Markdown-first protocol, but it may belong as a project principle rather than a general concept note.

Related:

- [[docs/adr/0005-treat-markdown-as-the-codebase]]
- [[docs/adr/0008-allow-attachments-with-markdown-records]]

## Epistemic Status

Metadata that tells readers how mature, certain, or effortful a note is.

Suggested promotion: not yet. It is interesting for future schema design, but premature until we decide whether maturity metadata is part of this protocol.

## Knowledge Gardener

- Status: promoted to [[wiki/concepts/knowledge-gardener]]

An archetype for a person or agent who shapes conditions for knowledge growth through planting, pruning, linking, tending, and harvesting rather than imposing a rigid hierarchy.

Suggested promotion: maybe. It captures the user's preferred relationship to knowledge engineering, but may need one more packet or reflection before becoming trusted.

Related:

- [[wiki/synthesis/llm-wiki-pattern]]

## Playful Knowledge Practice

- Status: promoted to [[wiki/concepts/playful-knowledge-practice]]

The principle that a knowledge system should preserve play, curiosity, and lightness so that learning remains sustainable rather than collapsing into self-optimization ritual.

Suggested promotion: maybe. It is important for product design and failure-mode prevention, but may belong first as a project principle.

Related:

- [[docs/context/user-working-profile]]

## Maturity Metaphor

A metaphorical status vocabulary for evolving notes, such as seedling, budding, evergreen, pruning, or composting.

Suggested promotion: not yet. Worth testing, but risky if it creates too much ritual or maintenance burden.
