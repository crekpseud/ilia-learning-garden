# Gravity Map

This is a two-week experiment in whole-garden sense-making.

The map tracks which ideas currently exert the strongest pull on the garden. It is not a ranking of importance, truth, recency, or promotion priority. It is a gardener's weather report: where attention is bending, where concepts are forming constellations, and where an attractive metaphor might become a trap.

The better embodied metaphor is closer to forest bathing than physics. Enter the garden, get quiet, notice what pulls attention, and move a little in that direction. The pull does not need to prove it is objectively most important. It only needs to feel alive, unresolved, connected, or strangely recurring.

There is also a possible Heideggerian root here: thinking as being underway on paths, forest paths, country paths, and clearings. The garden may not only contain concepts; it may disclose paths when the gardener attends to what is opening.

## How To Read Gravity

An idea has gravity when it:

- appears across multiple packets or trusted notes
- attracts new questions
- changes how older notes read
- creates next learning paths
- carries strong human excitement or correction
- connects personal learning with software design
- risks becoming too beautiful, broad, or ritualized

## False Gravity Watch

These ideas feel powerful. For the next week or two, treat them as beautiful but suspicious until they earn their keep.

### Abstraction As Gravity

Why suspicious:

- It is elegant enough to over-explain everything.

Test:

- Can it predict useful distinctions between REST, Docker, Kubernetes, MCP, and skills, or does it merely redescribe them?

### Mycelium And Garden Metaphors

Why suspicious:

- They are deeply aligned with the garden, but can become aesthetic glue.

Test:

- Do they change an actual protocol decision or learning behavior?

### Protocol Expansion

Why suspicious:

- The garden has become good at inventing artifacts.

Test:

- Does each new artifact reduce cognitive load or increase it?
