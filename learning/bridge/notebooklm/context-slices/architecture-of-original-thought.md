# Vault Context Slice - Architecture of Original Thought

## Protocol Summary

- Markdown is the durable protocol.
- The vault is read topographically through links, concepts, maps, and synthesis notes.
- Workflow history is append-only in logs, packet reviews, and Git.
- Trusted notes change through reviewed patches, not silent rewrites.
- Candidate concepts are suggestions only. Concept promotion requires human approval.
- A packet normally promotes one synthesis note by default, plus zero or more approved reusable concept notes.
- The external LLM produces advisory packet output only. Local reconciliation, review, and promotion happen later.
- The human-contact gate is essential: knowledge must pass through the learner's own attention before it enters the trusted wiki.

## Topic Guidance

This packet should explore the architecture of original thought and effective learning.

Focus on:

- How independent thought is built through active reconstruction rather than passive consumption.
- Bloom's taxonomy and whether it can help this protocol distinguish surface familiarity from mastery.
- Adler's levels of reading and whether they can guide source engagement inside learning packets.
- First-principles reasoning and its relationship to asking better questions before synthesis.
- Desirable difficulties, testing, active recall, and spaced repetition as friction that improves durable learning.
- The risk of outsourcing intellectual effort to AI assistants or external tools.
- How AI can scaffold learning without replacing the learner's own reconstruction.
- How this topic should influence the human-contact gate and future learning packet design.

Pay special attention to tensions:

- When does AI help original thought, and when does it flatten it?
- How much difficulty should the protocol preserve instead of smoothing away?
- Should quizzes, teach-backs, or first-principles summaries become stronger human-contact artifact types?
- How can the garden remain playful while still requiring real cognitive work?
